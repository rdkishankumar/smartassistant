## 2. What Is Generative AI?

### Definition
- **Generative AI (Gen AI)** = A super smart artist or writer that uses computers to **create new things**.
- Creates: Stories, images, videos, ideas, responses to questions.
- Analogy: A robot that draws pictures or writes poems all by itself.

### Context in Broader Tech
- Part of a larger "family" of technologies that help computers **think and learn**.
- Family includes: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Neural Networks, NLP, Gen AI, Large Language Models.

---

## 3. The Timeline: Evolution to Generative AI

### Historical Development
- Generative AI didn't evolve overnight — it's the result of decades of progress.
- **1950s**: Artificial Intelligence (AI) emerged as a computer science field.
- **1990s**: Machine Learning (ML) evolved as a subset of AI.
- **~2010**: Deep Learning (DL) emerged as a subset of ML.
- **~2020s**: Generative AI (Gen AI) emerged as a subset of DL.

---

## 4. The Family Tree of AI

### Hierarchical Structure
```
Artificial Intelligence (Top Level / Grandparent)
└── Machine Learning (Subset of AI)
    └── Deep Learning (Subset of ML)
        └── Generative AI (Subset of DL)
            ├── Large Language Models (LLMs) → Focus on text generation
            ├── Image Generation Models → Focus on images
            ├── Video Generation Models → Focus on videos
            ├── Code Generation Models → Focus on code
            └── Other Models → Various outputs
```

### Key Points
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: The overarching field for machines that think.
- **Machine Learning (ML)**: Machines learn from data.
- **Deep Learning (DL)**: Uses complex neural networks.
- **Generative AI (Gen AI)**: Creates new content; includes specialized models for different outputs.

---

## 5. Parallel Fields That Evolved Alongside AI

### Data Science
- Combines: Mathematics, statistics, programming, and domain knowledge.
- Purpose: Extract meaningful insights from data.
- Primary Use: **Predictions based on historical data** (e.g., forecasting sales, trends).

### Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Enables human-computer interaction using **natural language** (e.g., English, Hindi).
- Powers understanding of instructions given to AI (e.g., when you ask questions to generative AI).
- Behind-the-scenes: Converts natural language into machine-understandable formats.
